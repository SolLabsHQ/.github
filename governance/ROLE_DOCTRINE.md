# Role Doctrine

This document defines the core leadership roles of the organization.
Titles are not ornamental. They exist to make authority, responsibility,
and decision boundaries explicit.

---

## Founder & Chief Architect

**Purpose**  
Design, integrate, and evolve the organizationâ€™s core systems.

**Authority**
- Final authority over system architecture (technical, conceptual, strategic)
- Authority to introduce new systems or retire broken ones
- Tie-breaker when multiple options compete structurally

**Responsibilities**
- Define long-term architectural vision
- Translate vision into durable, operable systems
- Maintain coherence across products, platforms, and processes
- Push innovation without compromising integrity
- Protect the system from short-term thinking and architectural drift

**Guiding Question**  
> Can this system scale, endure, and still make sense over time?

---

## Chief of Practical Governance

**Purpose**  
Ensure decisions and systems make sense to intelligent, reasonable humans.

**Authority**
- Authority to question, pause, or stop initiatives that fail the common-sense test
- Equal standing in governance discussions with the Chief Architect
- Veto power on decisions that introduce unnecessary complexity or human harm

**Responsibilities**
- Evaluate clarity, explainability, and real-world practicality
- Detect over-engineering, ego-driven design, and abstraction drift
- Translate complexity into human-legible terms
- Act as a grounding force when ambition outpaces reality
- Protect the organization from self-deception

**Guiding Question**  
> Would this make sense to a smart person who is not emotionally invested in the idea?
